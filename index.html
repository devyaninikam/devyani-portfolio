About Me

My name is Devyani Nikam, and I am currently pursuing my B.Ed with a specialization in Science education. I have always been fascinated by how scientific principles shape the world around us, and this curiosity naturally evolved into a desire to teach.

During my teaching practice, I realized that every child learns differently. This helped me develop a teaching approach that is inclusive, interactive, and rooted in real-life examples. I enjoy creating engaging teaching aids, digital presentations, and activity-based learning experiences for my students.

Apart from teaching, I have a creative side, I love painting, and I find joy in making DIY crafts. These hobbies help me stay patient, observant, and imaginative—qualities that deeply influence my teaching style.

I aspire to be a teacher who not only imparts knowledge but also inspires students to become independent thinkers and lifelong learners.

My Philosophy

My teaching philosophy is inspired by John Dewey's belief that learning grows from real experiences—an idea that deeply resonates with my own journey as a learner and teacher. I strive to create a classroom where students explore, question, and learn by doing, just as I always loved to.

Through hands-on activities, technology, and inquiry-based tasks, I aim to make science something students can feel, observe, and connect to their daily lives. For me, teaching is not just sharing knowledge but nurturing curious, confident learners who reflect, grow, and find joy in understanding the world around them.

Research Work

Teaching Styles Among Pre-service Teachers

This research explored the different teaching styles preferred and practiced by pre-service teachers during their training period. Using a survey-based approach, the study examined four major teaching styles—authoritative, facilitative, delegator, and demonstrator. The findings revealed that most pre-service teachers favored a facilitative and student-centered approach, influenced by modern pedagogical training and the emphasis on activity-based learning under NEP 2020.

Key Insights:

  • Teaching styles shaped by internship experiences and mentor feedback
  • Classroom environment and self-reflection play crucial roles
  • Need for exposure to diverse teaching models
  • Importance of developing flexible and adaptive teaching approaches

The research highlighted how teaching styles are shaped by internship experiences, mentor feedback, classroom environment, and self-reflection. It also suggested the need for continuous exposure to varied teaching models to help student-teachers develop a flexible, adaptive, and inclusive teaching style that meets the diverse needs of learners.
